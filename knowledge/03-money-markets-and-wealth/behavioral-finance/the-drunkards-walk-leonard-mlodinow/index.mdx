_The Drunkard's Walk_ is Leonard Mlodinow's 2008 popular science book that became a _New York Times_ bestseller and won the Robert P. Balles Prize for Critical Thinking from the Committee for Skeptical Inquiry. Drawing on his background as a theoretical physicist and his gift for narrative storytelling, Mlodinow traces the history of probability theory from Cardano and Pascal through Laplace and Bernoulli, then applies its principles to everyday puzzles: why wine ratings are unreliable, why political polls have margins of error, why CEOs get credit for luck, and why the Monty Hall problem confounds nearly everyone. The book's central message is both humbling and liberating — randomness rules far more of our lives than we are willing to admit, and recognizing this is the first step toward clearer thinking about success, failure, and the uncertain world of financial markets.
